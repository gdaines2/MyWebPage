Dinosaur Facts

  1. What are Dinosaurs: Dinosaurs were a diverse group of reptiles that dominated the Earth for over 160 million years during the Mesozoic Era, from about 230 to 65 million years ago.
  2. Types of Dinosaurs: There were two main groups of dinosaurs: Saurischia (lizard-hipped) and Ornithischia (bird-hipped). These classifications are based on the structure of their pelvic bones.
  3. Extinction: The majority of dinosaurs went extinct around 65 million years ago, likely due to a catastrophic event, such as an asteroid impact or volcanic activity. This event marked the end of the Mesozoic Era and the beginning of the Cenozoic Era.

Brachiosaurus

Brachiosaurus is a genus of sauropod dinosaur that lived in North America during the Late Jurassic, about 154 to 150 million years ago.

Tylosaurus

Tylosaurus is a genus of mosasaur, a large, predatory marine reptile closely related to modern monitor lizards and snakes, from the Late Cretaceous.

Stegosaurus

Stegosaurus is a genus of herbivorous, four-legged, armored dinosaur from the Late Jurassic, characterized by the distinctive kite-shaped upright plates along their backs and spikes on their tails.
